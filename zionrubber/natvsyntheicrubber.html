Which is Better Natural Bubber or Synthetic Rubber?

When purchasing rubber flooring, industrial mats, gym flooring, or livestock mats, one of the most common questions is whether natural rubber or synthetic rubber is the better choice. The answer depends on where the product will be used, but understanding the differences can help you make the right investment.At Zion Rubber we have ant extensive collection of both Natural and Synthetic Rubber Products.

What is Natural Rubber

Natural rubber is harvested from the latex of the Hevea brasiliensis (rubber tree). It is a renewable material that has been trusted for decades due to its exceptional elasticity, strength, and durability.

Natural rubber is widely used in:

  • Gym flooring
  • Cow and horse stable mats
  • Industrial flooring
  • Anti-fatigue mats
  • Playground flooring
  • Heavy-duty commercial applications

What is Synthetic Rubber

Synthetic rubber is manufactured from petroleum-based chemicals. Different types of synthetic rubber are engineered for specific applications where resistance to oils, chemicals, or extreme weather is important.

Common synthetic rubber types include:

  • SBR (Styrene-Butadiene Rubber)
  • EPDM
  • Nitrile Rubber (NBR)
  • Neoprene

Why Natural Rubber is Preferred for Flooring

For gyms, livestock farms, factories, schools, warehouses, and commercial spaces, natural rubber offers several advantages.

Common synthetic rubber types include:

  • Superior Shock Absorption
  • Excellent Slip Resistance
  • High Load Bearing Capacity
  • Long Service Life
  • Environmentally Friendly
